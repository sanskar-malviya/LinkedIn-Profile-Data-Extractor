    ⚠️ Delivery Scam Alert – Please Read Before Your Next Food Order ⚠️

    Recently I had a concerning experience while receiving a food order from     Zomato.

    The delivery partner arrived at my home and delivered the order normally. After handing over the food, he requested help saying his bike tyre had punctured and asked if I could send him ₹50 so he could manage the situation.

    Since he was already at my doorstep and looked genuine, I agreed to help. But while making the payment he increased the amount and I ended up sending ₹100 via UPI. He assured me that he would refund the money the next morning.

    However, the refund never came.

    After a day I realized something important — customers are not supposed to pay any extra money to delivery partners outside the app. If a delivery partner faces any issue during delivery, the company has proper support systems for them.

    I raised a complaint with Zomato and shared the proof (order details and UPI payment screenshot). After reviewing the case, Zomato investigated the issue and initiated a full refund of the ₹100.

    This is not about ₹100.
    It’s about awareness.

    Many customers might ignore small amounts and not report them, which allows such practices to continue.

    📌 Important things to remember:
    • Never send money directly to a delivery partner outside the app.
    • If someone asks for help, verify through the app or support.
    • Always keep payment proof and order details.
    • Report such incidents to customer support.

    While the resolution took a few days, I appreciate that the issue was eventually addressed.

    Sharing this so others stay aware and don’t fall for similar situations.
